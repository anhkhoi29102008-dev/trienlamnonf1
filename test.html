Lewis Hamilton

7× Formula 1 World Champion • Record race-winner • Activist & style icon

Lewis Carl Davidson Hamilton (born January 7, 1985) is a British racing driver widely considered one of the greatest Formula 1 competitors. He debuted in F1 with McLaren in 2007, won his first championship in 2008, and later joined Mercedes where he secured multiple world titles. Off-track, Hamilton is notable for his activism on diversity, sustainability, and social justice, and for his influence in fashion and culture.

Career Timeline — key milestones

1993–2000 — Early karting

Started karting at age 8 and progressed through junior categories quickly.

2001–2006 — Junior formula success

Strong results in Formula Renault and GP2 set him up for an F1 seat.

2007 — F1 debut with McLaren

Outstanding rookie season: race wins and multiple podiums.

2008 — First World Championship

Won his first Drivers' Championship in a dramatic season finale in Brazil.

2013 — Joined Mercedes

Moved to Mercedes-AMG Petronas and started a dominant era for the team.

2014–2020 — Multiple World Championships

Secured six more titles during a period of Mercedes dominance.

2021 — Historic title battle

Contested a tightly fought championship season that drew global attention.

2022–2025 — Continued legacy

Remains a central figure in F1, advocating for social causes and influencing motorsport culture.
